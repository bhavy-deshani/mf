Ways to save up for your child’s education

Among the top stressors for parents in India today is the rising cost of educating their children. Public educational institutions lack quality of teaching and infrastructure. While private educational institutions offer quality on both fronts, they are very expensive. Yet, as a recent study of parents in India found, 73% of the parents polled preferred to send their children to a private institution.

The cost of educating children is not just limited to sending them to a premium school. Additionally, parents must plan to spend on extra academic coaching classes, sports and arts classes, travel for experiential learning and other educational accessories and gadgets. On an average, parents end up investing between Rs.20 lakh and Rs.30 lakh on a single child to get them through primary, middle and high school.

Even as these costs look big, schools are announcing a hike in their annual fees. One survey puts the increase at between 30% and 60%. Transport operators too have increased their charges. And the costs of books, stationery and other school-related accessories have also gone up.

So do parents cope with these rising costs? How do they plan to invest smartly so that the education of their children is secure – and the costs can be met? Parents must take a hard look at securing the education of their children. They need to plan financially on this front. There are three areas of focus.

Focus area 1

Choosing sound investments and investing right is the first step. These sound investments are those that are insulated from the impact of inflation and yet provide good returns. Equity-based mutual funds are a good option to consider. They are managed by professionals, they have the risk spread across wide-ranging assets and the fund managers often diversify. So, these investments are reasonably safe. Systematic investments plans, or SIPs, offered by mutual funds allow parents to invest smaller sums monthly. Over time, these investments grow into a large corpus that is available to parents to fund the higher education needs of their children. In addition to mutual funds, there are public, government-approved investment options too available.

Focus area 2

The second way to invest in education for children is to take education loans. This step may be taken if the investments made are insufficient. But choosing the right loan is important. Most education loans come with tax benefits. The interest amount paid on the loan can be claimed as a deduction against income tax. Again, parents must plan well and understand what the impact of a long-term borrowing like an education loan is. Some loans come for a tenure of around eight years. So careful thought and consideration must go into choosing to go for such loans.

Focus area 3

Parents must look for grants and scholarships that are available for the fields of study and areas of interest that their children have chosen. Diligent research on the internet can point parents to these funding opportunities. And if the child is meeting all eligibility criteria, these grants and scholarship can be secured seamlessly. While such funding may not bridge the entire cost of education, it will help lessen the financial pressure on parents to a significant extent.

Think ahead with an open mind

Most parents tend to worry about the rising costs and whether they will at all be able to fund the education of their children. Worrying does not help anyone. Parents must instead keep an open mind and think of the journey ahead practically.

Here are some tips:

1. Do not imagine that your child should do this professional course or that even when they are still in primary or middle school. As children grow, their interests change. Their skills also develop. So don’t plan to make your child a doctor or an engineer or a lawyer or a cricketer or a musician or a filmmaker. Not when they are still in school.

2. Instead invest a certain sum every month in a fund for your child’s future education. As the child grows, the fund will grow too. Do not touch this fund for any other purpose. Whatever be the emergency do not draw from this fund. When the child goes to high school and begins to show an aptitude for a particular field of study, you may unlock this fund and invest the amount on their education.

3. You may actively solicit the support of your extended family if they plan to gift you child more toys and gadgets than what they have currently. You may advise them to invest in the fund. This includes grandparents, uncles and aunts. And close friends of the family.

Conclusion

Parenting is a great opportunity to raise a talented, responsible and caring human being. Do not waste this opportunity worrying about your child. Do not also limit your child’s interests and talent by imagining that you cannot provide for them financially.

With some discipline and focus, you can plan early and plan well.
