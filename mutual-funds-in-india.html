Types of Mutual Funds in India

Mutual funds are a very common investment option these days. A mutual fund gives individual investors access to diversified, professionally managed portfolios.

What is a mutual fund?

A mutual fund is an investment vehicle that pools money from multiple investors to purchase a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds or other securities. A mutual fund is known by the kind of securities that they invest in, their investment objectives and the type of returns that they seek. A fund manager is responsible and keeps an eye out and manages these pooled investments. Through mutual funds, individual investors get exposure to portfolios that are managed by experts.

Types of mutual Funds

There is a diverse range of mutual funds available that are tailored to suit specific investment goals. Mutual funds are classified based on:

• Organization structure

• Asset classes

• Investment objectives

• Portfolio management

• Speciality

• Risk appetite

Let us look at them in detail.

Mutual funds based on organizational structure

1. Close-ended schemes: These funds come with a fixed maturity period. The units of these schemes are issued by fund houses only at the time of their New Fund Offer (NFO). After the NFO period, these schemes are listed on the stock exchanges. An investor wanting to leave these schemes before the maturity period, can trade their units on the stock exchange. After maturity, the schemes are dissolved and the investors receive the money back the Net Asset Value (NAV). The NAV represents the market value per share of any mutual fund. Close-ended mutual funds are ideal for those who are looking for a long-term investment. In other words, people who have a hefty sum and are seeking an avenue to park the funds for an extended period of time will benefit from these types of mutual funds.

2. Open-ended schemes: Unlike close-ended schemes, investors can buy and sell units of these schemes even after the NFO. This is an ideal option for investors who want to buy and sell the units at their convenience. Investors can sell the units at the NAV of the day. This offers a higher level of liquidity to investors who want to enter and exit these plans and are willing to take a moderate or high risk.

3. Interval schemes: These are a hybrid option of both the above types that we have discussed. The units of these schemes can be bought and sold only during specific transaction periods called intervals. As is done in close-ended schemes, the units of the interval schemes are listed on stock exchanges. Investors who are seeking exposure to unconventional assets like commercial property or forestry tracts can benefit from these type of funds.

Mutual fund schemes based on asset classes

• Equity funds: These funds invest the assets in the shares of companies. An equity fund scheme invests at least 65% of their assets in equities and related investments. The balance funds are invested in more secure assets to offset any risk involved. The performance of the shares dictate the returns from these funds. Furthermore, equity funds can be categorized in many ways. For instance, they can be based on the way they are managed: as active or passive funds. Sometimes people categorize them based on the market capitalization of the stocks that they invest in: so either small, mid, multi or large cap funds. They can also be categorized on the geography that they invest in (domestic and foreign funds) or the sector in which they invest in (pharma, FMCG, real estate). Equity funds have a track record of good returns when compared with other mutual funds and are suitable for building wealth.

• Debt funds: These funds invest their wealth in fixed-income instruments like corporate or government bonds or treasury bills. Debt funds are less risky when compared to equity funds. They offer better returns even when compared to traditional options like fixed deposits. If you are seeking a regular income, this is a good option.

• Hybrid funds: Hybrid funds consist of both debt and equity components. Those with a moderate risk appetite can go for this option. Hybrid funds are classified based on how they invest their funds. They can be classified as conservative (invest 75% of asset in debt and the rest in equity), aggressive (invest 65-80% in equity and the rest in debt instruments), dynamic asset allocation funds (invest in both debt and equity based on market conditions), multi asset allocation (invest at least 10% of the money in three asset classes based on market conditions), arbitrage funds (invest 65% of asset in equity and balance in debt) and lastly equity savings funds (invest at least 65% of the asset in equity and 10% in debt instruments).

• Money market funds: These funds normally invest in low-risk, short term securities such as treasury bills, certificates of deposit and commercial paper. Since they offer high liquidity, investors use them as an effective short-term cash management tool.

Mutual fund schemes based on portfolio management

• Active funds: The portfolio is actively managed by the fund managers who decide

which securities to buy and sell. These funds achieve higher returns through active management strategies and are designed to outperform a benchmark index.

• Passive funds: These are designed to replicate the performance of a particular market index. They match the returns of the index that they track. Since they do not involve active stock selection or market timing strategies, they are termed “passive”.

Mutual fund schemes based on investment objective

• Growth funds: These funds invest in stocks of companies that expect to register above-average growth rate compared to their industry peers. These mutual funds provide high returns and also carry a higher risk.

• Income funds: These funds provide a steady income stream through interest payments or dividends. These funds invest in assets that generate income like bonds, stocks that pay dividends, and other fixed-income securities. These funds are suitable for people who are retiring or those looking to supplement their income.

• Liquid funds: These funds invest in short-term debt instruments with low risk and high liquidity. These funds provide a safe and liquid investment option with an option to convert the funds into cash without too much losses.

Mutual fund schemes based on investment objective

• Low-risk funds: These funds invest in debt instruments and offer stable returns rather than aggressive growth.

• Medium-risk funds: They invest in a diversified portfolio of assets and have a moderate level of risk. They invest in a mix of stocks, bond and financial instruments. They are a good balance between good returns and risk exposure.

• High-risk funds: These funds have high-risk assets such as stocks, commodities, futures or emerging markets. They generate high returns and are ideal for wealth creation.

Mutual fund schemes based on investment objective

These funds focus on specific industries, themes or sectors. They aim to capitalise on the growth of a particular sector by investing in companies in that domain. Examples are:

• Sector funds: These funds invest in a particular industry. The performance of these sectors will dictate the returns from these funds. Examples are technology or real estate funds.

• Index funds: They invest in a diversifies portfolio of stocks that comprise the chosen index and in the same proportion of the index. These are passive funds.

• Global funds: These funds allow Indian investors to invest in foreign securities and markets.

• Fund of funds: These invest in a portfolio of other mutual funds. The funds aim to spread the investment risk and achieve higher returns.

• Retirement funds: These funds are designed to help those who are accumulating wealth of their retirement years. They invest the pooled money in stocks, bonds and securities.

• Emerging market funds: These funds invest in emerging market economies. These economies have rapid growth, expanding population and increased industrialization. These funds help investors participate in the growth of these economies.

Conclusion

Mutual funds are ideal for wealth creation. Various mutual funds are available and can cater to every need of an investor. It is important to analyse the performance of a mutual fund and choose one that suits your requirement, financial goals and risk appetite. It is important to read the policy document carefully to know where the funds are invested and understand the terms and conditions of the mutual fund.
