Capital Gain Bonds

Floating Rate Savings Bonds: Those desirous of availing exemption from capital gains tax under Section 54 EC may invest the sale proceeds in the capital gain bonds. The investment is to be made within a period of six months from the date of transfer/sale of the asset to get exemption from the capital gains tax. This exemption is available for long term capital gains i.e. the profits made from sale of assets after 3 years of acquisition.

What is Corporate Fixed Deposit?

A Corporate Fixed Deposit (FD) is an investment option regulated by the RBI, provided by Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs), offering a fixed interest rate over a specified tenure. Investors can choose to receive their earnings either as regular income through monthly, quarterly, or annual payouts, or as a lump sum upon maturity through a cumulative payout option.

Interest Rates: Rates are subject to change and are typically determined on the date of the deposit. It's recommended to verify the current rates with the branch team before investing.

Key Features of Corporate Fixed Deposits

Tenure

The deposit period ranges from 1 to 5 years.

Interest Payout Options

Investors can choose from monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or yearly interest payouts based on their preferences.

Risk

Since these are unsecured deposits, it's crucial for investors to thoroughly assess the financial health of the company before investing.

Understanding Debentures

When a company needs to raise funds from the public, one of the common methods is by issuing a debt instrument known as a debenture. A debenture is a type of bond that the company issues for a specified tenure, offering a fixed interest rate to the investor over the period.

Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs):

  • These are debentures that cannot be converted into equity shares of the issuing company.
  • NCDs may or may not be secured. A secured NCD is backed by the company's assets.

Credit Rating:

  • A company willing to raise money shall get its issue rated by credit rating agencies like CRISIL, ICRA, CARE, India Rating, Brickwork etc.
