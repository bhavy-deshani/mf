What is Equity? How Do I Get Started?

Most of you must have heard stories about how certain stocks have multiplied manifold over the last 10 or 15 years. Many investors talk about how they have made fortunes in the equity markets. Before we jump to conclusions, it is important to understand that equity investment is riskier and more volatile when compared to traditional forms of investments like fixed deposits or bonds. However, people prefer them because they have the potential for higher returns. The Sensex has grown phenomenally in the last 40 to 50 years and those who stuck on made profits. Equity investing calls for planning, analysis and the ability to stay put in the long term. What is equity?

Equity is also referred to as shares and stocks of a company. Therefore, when you buy equity, you become part owners of the company. In broad terms, equity refers to the amount of money that would be returned to a company’s shareholders should all the assets be liquidated and all the company’s debt were paid off. These shares are traded on the stock market. So, when you own equity in a company, you receive returns in the form of dividends that the company distributes and you also get capital gains when the stock price goes above the purchase price. In short, when you own equity, you have ownership. The owner or the equity shareholder receives high returns along with a high risk as well.

Key aspects of equity trading

Before we understand how to invest in equity, let us understand certain terms related to equity.

1. Shares: A share is a unit of ownership in a specific company. For instance, you could say that you won 10 shares in a company X.

2. Stocks: Stocks is a term that is used to refer to portions of ownership in multiple companies. For example, you could say that you own stocks in Company X and Company Y.

3. Equity: Equity is a term used when you want to denote the total ownership stake in a company. For example, if a company has 10,000 shares, and you owned 1000 of them, you could say that you own 10% equity stake in that company.

4. Stock market: An exchange mechanism that helps investors buy and sell shares, bonds, and securities in publicly traded companies. In this platform, buyers and sellers come together to trade financial tools during specific hours of the business day. They have to adhere to guidelines laid out by the Securities and exchange Board of India (SEBI). Trades are done electronically.

5. Broker: Individual investors who typically execute equity trades through brokerage firms. These firms act as intermediaries and facilitate the buying and selling of stocks on behalf of their clients.

6. Indices: Indices refer to a standardized way to track the performance of a group of assets or stocks. Indices such as Nifty 50 and Sensex, are benchmarks that represent the overall performance of the stock market. The indices are composed of a basket of stocks and serve as indicators of market trends.

7. Regulatory bodies: SEBI is the regulatory body for securities and commodities market in India. SEBI ensures fair practices and investor protection in the capital markets.

8. Investor types: There are different types of investors in the equity market like retail investors (individuals), institutional investors (mutual funds, banks and insurance companies) and foreign institutional investors (FIIs).

9. Settlement process: The settlement process involves the transfer of shares and funds between buyers and sellers. In India, the settlement cycle is normally T + 1, which means the transaction settles one business day after the trade date.

10. Risk and reward: Every equity investor knows that equity trading involves risk. The prices can be influenced by various factors such as company performance, economic conditions, global events, and market sentiment. Investors aim to profit from price fluctuations and dividend income.

How to invest in equity?

To begin trading in equity, you need to follow the following steps:

Step 1: Find a stockbroker

Find a reliable stock broker. To begin with, you will have to open a demat account and a trading account with a broker firm to invest in the stock market. A trading account is for transactions and demat account is for holding the shares. It is mandatory to have both these accounts. Ideally, take advise from a financial advisor who can guide you on what to buy and purchase the funds for you. The other option is to purchase the equity funds from a fund house directly.

Step 2: Open a Demat and trading account

Simple steps can be followed to open a Demat account:

• Visit the link for account opening

• Enter KYC details like name, email, PAN and date of birth

• Provide address and bank account details

• Upload documents related to proof of identity and proof of address

• Choose a subscription plan

• Complete e-sign by submitting the OTP sent to the mobile number registered with your Aadhar card

You will now receive login credentials for your Demat and trading account. Now you are ready to start trading online.

Step 3: Log in and add funds

Once you have your log in credentials, log into your Demat and trading account. Now you can add funds to your account.

You can transfer funds using:

• A payment gateway

• You could use RTGS/ NEFT/ IMPS

• You could also use a cheque or DD to transfer funds

Step 4: View stock details and start trading

Log in and explore the share market on your Demat and trading account. Analyse stock prices, check patterns, and observe price movements using charts and other tools. Select the stocks that you want to trade and place your order.

Key benefits of investing in equity shares

• Equities are a reliable asset and create wealth in the long-term.

• When you invest in a portfolio of about 8 to 10 stocks, you can diversify the risk. You can also consider investing in a equity fund. This is a mutual fund that invests in equities.

• You can monitor your portfolio value live on your trading platform 24*7.

• The tax component on equities are lower than that of fixed deposits or bonds.

Risks of investing in the stock market

Investing in the stock market can be volatile and unpredictable in the short term and more consistent in the long term. Ensure that you buy quality stocks or rely on equity mutual funds. It is important to be patient with equities and not expect miracles to happen. Stock markets are affected by various things like global events or market conditions which are out of your control. Therefore ensure that you have a diverse portfolio.

Conclusion

Equity trading is an attractive investment. Investors must approach the market with a clear understanding of the risks involved, their own risk tolerance, investment goals and a well thought out strategy. It is important to stay informed of market trends and company performance and make prudent investment decisions.
