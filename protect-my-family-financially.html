How Can I Protect My Family Financially?

Every human being wants the very best for their family. The safety and security of the family forms the primary moving force in anyone’s life. Financial stability and safety ensure a secure environment, giving the family the best atmosphere to be nurtured in and grow in a healthy manner.

However, life is turbulent and it is not possible to know from where the next curve ball will hit you. You could have a health setback in the family or a sudden crisis like having to care for an ailing parent or maybe an accident. In any situation where a crisis erupts, the first thing that comes into the picture is your financial position and ability to handle the crisis efficiently. Of course it is also important to be strong and address the issue on hand with a calm frame of mind. However, being financially prepared is a huge advantage and can help deal with problems in a more confident manner.

Planning finances should be a family priority. It is important to involve your spouse. Consider if you are a sole bread winner, or a family with double income. Plan and make wise investments. See where you can save and how you can maintain the current lifestyle that you have if something happens to one of the spouses. Both spouses must be involved in financial decisions to ensure that if there is a crisis, both know how to access the funds to deal with a situation on hand.

There are various things that you can do to plan your finances and secure the future of your family. Let us understand the steps and where you must begin.

1. Planning your finances well

Life is itself unpredictable. Therefore, despite being well-planned, you may face a financial setback. As a first step, it is important to learn to manage your finances well. To begin with, start by setting a financial goal. Grab a piece of paper and start planning. Answer the following questions:

• Where do you see yourself/ your family in 5, 10 or 15 years?

• What is your daily lifestyle? What do you want it to look like in the future?

• What are the assets that you wish to acquire/ build?

• When do you want to retire? How would you want to plan your retirement?

• How do expect your career to grow in 5, 10 and 15 years’ time?

• What goals to do you have for your children?

2. Get organized

Once the visioning and goal setting is done, get down to work. Things that you can start doing immediately:

• Make a list and review the financial accounts you have very carefully. Savings accounts, salary accounts, credit cards and so on. Keep a track of your balances in a spreadsheet. Store the passwords carefully in a password wallet.

• Make a list of all your debts, small and large and the interest rates that you are paying.

• Comb through your bank statements and credit card statements. Create an itemized monthly budget and optimize your money for higher savings.

3. Pay down your debts

Bad debt has an ability to hold your future finances down powerfully. So, look through your debts whether it is a student loan, credit card, or car loan. Paying off the debt as soon as possible should be a priority. Make every effort to cut down expenses and pay off the debt. Many people pay the minimum amount every month and push the ball to later. This is the worst thing you can do.

4. Invest in an insurance plan

Insurance plans give a lot of comfort especially at times when something happens all of a sudden. Life is uncertain and it is important to provide cushions that could be useful in the time of a crisis.

There are various insurance products available in the market that you could choose from. Health insurance is critical for every family. A sudden health setback or a surprise surgery could disrupt your finances suddenly. This is where health insurance provides the comfort and cover that could help immensely. You could Also consider a long-term disability insurance. This comes in handy when you have a serious illness or injury that prevents you from working for a long period of time like a couple of years. How will you survive without an income? A long-term disability plan ensures that you get paid. The other insurance that one must consider seriously is life insurance. You can take it for both you and your spouse so that should something happen to either of you, the family is compensated. Ideally it is good to take ten times of your annual income as the amount to be insured.

5. Invest in quality education for children

Your children’s education must be a top priority when you are doing your financial planning. Enabling your children with the best education possible is the best gift you can give them. Invest in tuition classes, reference books, and online learning modules that will help your children perform better. It is also important to educate your children about financial management. The earlier they learn to earn and save, the better they can be prepared to become responsible adults themselves. Get them started young with the habit of saving.

6. Plan for your estate

Anyone and everyone must have a plan for their estate irrespective of its size. This must include a will, testament, and a power of attorney. A will is the best way to safeguard your family’s future in the years to come. If you die without making a will, your money, property and possessions will be allocated according to law which not be according to your wishes. In your will, you can make an inventory of your financial assets such as bank accounts, and life insurance policies so that the executors of your will know where to find these after you have passed away.

7. Research on tax savings

Take assistance from a financial planning advisor and examine ways in which you can save on tax payments. There are various investment options that can be helpful. Financial advisors can also help with ideas to boost financial security. Using professional help is important to achieve your financial goals.

8. Keep your documents safe

It is very important to keep all financial documents of account details and insurance policies in a safe place where your family can easily access them. You could choose a safe place at home or even a safe deposit locker to store these. Ensure that you keep your family informed of where you have stored them. These days there are various options to store them on cloud as well. Ensure that your family has the passwords in order to access them.

Summary

There is no formula for building and sustaining wealth. Everyone has to work on it themselves and do what works at an individual level. The situations and circumstances that each individual goes through dictates their path. So do your research and ensure that you plan and execute things as well as you can.
